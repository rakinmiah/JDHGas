# Location × Service Pages — SEO Strategy (JDH Gas)

_Focused sub-strategy on top of the existing `seo/` plan. Scope: up to 130 pages
(26 towns × 5 services). Prepared June 2026. Author: SEO/strategy pass via /plan-seo._

---

## 0. THE VERDICT — read this first

**Doing 130 templated "service in [town]" pages the naive way is the single most
dangerous thing we could do to this site.** It is, almost word-for-word, the
pattern Google's spam policy names and penalises. Done wrong it doesn't just fail
to rank — it can drag the **whole domain** (including the Burgess Hill rankings we
already have) down.

It **can** be done safely and win big — but only if:
1. Every page is **genuinely useful to a real person in that town**, not a town-swapped clone.
2. We **phase the rollout** (a few excellent pages first, prove them, then expand) — never publish 130 at once.
3. We are **honest** about being a Burgess-Hill-based service-area business (no fake "we're based in Hove").
4. We **gate expansion on evidence** (indexation + rankings) with a defined pause/kill switch.

**Volume is the enemy. Usefulness is the defence.** The rest of this document is
how we get the upside without the catastrophe.

## 1. What the research establishes (grounded)

### Google's policy — verbatim (developers.google.com/search spam policies)
- **Doorway abuse:** _"sites or pages created to rank for specific, similar search queries. They lead users to intermediate pages that are not as useful as the final destination."_ Named example: _"Having multiple domain names or **pages targeted at specific regions or cities** that funnel users to one page."_
- **Scaled content abuse:** _"many pages generated for the primary purpose of manipulating search rankings and not helping users."_

> Our 130-page idea is **exactly** this pattern *if* the pages are templated with the
> town swapped and they funnel to a generic enquiry. The defence is the inverse of
> both definitions: each page must be **the genuinely useful destination itself**, with
> **real per-town value**.

### Current best-practice consensus (multiple 2025–2026 sources, cited below)
- **Uniqueness floor:** each location page should be **≥60–70% unique**; **800–1,500 words**; **under 300 words = thin / penalty risk**.
- **The 70/30 model:** ~70% can be templated (what the service is, process, trust, CTA); **~30%+ must be genuinely location-specific and hand-shaped**.
- **Differentiation that counts:** area-specific testimonials, named neighbourhoods/landmarks, photos of work in the area, localised FAQs, common local issues, seasonal factors, local housing stock.
- **Quality > quantity:** _"15–20 exceptional city pages beat hundreds of thin, duplicate pages."_ This directly challenges "130 now."
- **The user test (apply to every page):** _"If a user lands here from search, do they find what they need — or is it a thin gateway forcing them to click elsewhere?"_

### SERP reality (the prize is real)
- Live UK SERP fetches were blocked by Google's consent wall (documented limitation — noted for honesty).
- **Real data we hold** (JDH SEO teardown, 22 Jun 2026, "boiler servicing Burgess Hill"): top results are **directories (Checkatrade #1, TrustATrader #5, MyBuilder #7)** + **established local firms (R G Shed #2, pdp.services #3, B.Cooke #6)**; JDH absent organically; a local map pack fires.
- **Implication:** these SERPs are beatable. Directories rank on authority, not depth — a genuinely useful local page from a named, Gas-Safe, well-reviewed local engineer can out-help them. But the bar is "genuinely useful per town," and the **map pack is proximity-gated** (see §6).

## 2. The safety doctrine (non-negotiable — every page, every wave)

1. **Destination, not gateway.** Each page must be the useful end point, answering the searcher fully. It must never exist only to funnel a click.
2. **The human test.** Before publishing: "would someone in this town find this genuinely useful and specific?" If no → don't ship it.
3. **Differentiate, don't swap.** Real local specifics, not one rewritten sentence with the town name.
4. **Phase, never flood.** Publish + index in waves; watch each wave before the next.
5. **Quality floor.** No page ships below the content/uniqueness bar (§3). A thin page is worse than no page — it taxes the whole domain.
6. **Never fabricate.** No invented testimonials, no fake "based in [town]", no made-up local jobs. We are a Burgess Hill service-area business; we say so.
7. **Real internal structure.** Hub → town → service linking (§5), never a 130-link footer dump.

## 3. The content model — what varies per page (the 70/30, made concrete)

Each **service-in-town page** is built from shared + unique blocks. The unique blocks
are the safety margin — they must be **substantive**, ideally **≥50% of the page** and
**400+ words of genuinely town/service-specific content**.

**SHARED (templated ~50–60%)** — fine to reuse, this is real info either way:
- What the service is + Jamie's process (drawn from the existing service pages)
- Gas Safe trust (reg. 977838), honest fixed-quote / £85 framing where relevant
- Service-specific FAQ (the existing ones)
- CTA, contact, reviews block (live Google rating)

**UNIQUE per town/service (the ~40–50% that earns the right to exist):**
- **Local intro** with real geography: where the town is, its postcode district (RH/BN), drive time from Burgess Hill, the **character of its housing stock** (e.g., Hove's Victorian terraces & conversion flats vs. Haywards Heath's mix of 1930s semis and new-builds vs. rural villages on LPG/oil).
- **Why this service matters *here*** — e.g., **CP12 in Hove/Portslade** (high rental/HMO density → landlord-heavy), **installs in rural villages** (LPG/oil considerations), **hard-water notes for the Sussex area** (scale → servicing relevance).
- **Real local proof when it exists** — a job done in/near that town, a review from that area (only if genuine; see §11 inputs).
- **A localised FAQ** — 1–2 town-specific Q&As ("Do you cover [neighbourhood]?", "How quickly can you get to [town] from Burgess Hill?").
- **Honest coverage framing** — "I'm based in Burgess Hill and regularly work across [town] and [nearby areas]."
- **Nearby-town cross-links** (real adjacency).

**Hard rule:** if we can't write 400+ words of genuinely town-specific, useful content
for a given town×service, that page **should not be built** (see §4 culling).

## 4. The hard truth about "130" + recommended phasing

**130 pages is too many to do well at once for a sole trader, and probably too many
full stop.** The consensus ("15–20 great pages beat hundreds of thin ones") and the
scaled-content policy both point the same way. So:

### Tiered, evidence-gated rollout
- **Tier 1 — Prove the model (~6–10 pages).** Top 3–5 towns × the 2 highest-intent services (**boiler servicing + landlord CP12**). Each page genuinely excellent. Ship → sitemap → GSC. **Wait 6–8 weeks.**
- **GATE:** Are they getting **indexed**? Ranking for the town×service term? Any GSC quality/coverage warnings? Burgess Hill rankings stable (no site-wide drop)?
- **Tier 2 — Expand in waves** (~10–15 pages per wave, every few weeks) only if the gate passes: more towns, then more services.
- **Tier 3 — Full coverage** only if quality holds and the model is demonstrably working.

### Prioritisation matrix (build order)
Score each town×service on **demand** (town size / search) × **genuine proximity & coverage** × **winnable competition**:
- **Towns first:** larger + closer win first — Haywards Heath, Hassocks, Lindfield, Cuckfield, Burgess Hill-adjacent. Bigger coastal towns (Hove, Portslade, Lancing) = more volume **but** harder map-pack (proximity) and more competition.
- **Services first:** **Boiler servicing** and **landlord CP12** (highest local commercial intent, clearest "service + town" search). Then repairs, installation, hob installs.

### Cull combos with no demand
Some of the 130 (e.g., "gas hob install in [tiny village]") have ~zero search and would
just be thin pages. **We should deliberately NOT build those** — fewer, real pages is
safer and stronger than forcing the full 26×5 grid.

## 5. Architecture & internal linking

Reintroduce `/areas` — but as **genuine, differentiated content**, not the thin version
we deleted earlier. Structure as a real hub-and-spoke (the opposite of a doorway):

- **Town hub:** `/areas/[town]` — a useful local landing page (coverage, the 5 services in that town, local map, local intro). Links to its service-in-town pages.
- **Service-in-town page:** `/areas/[town]/[service]` (e.g., `/areas/haywards-heath/boiler-servicing`).
  - _(Alternative URL pattern to decide: `/services/[service]/[town]`. Recommendation: `/areas/[town]/[service]` keeps the town as the local-intent anchor and groups cleanly. Decide in Phase A.)_
- **Link flow:** Homepage → `/areas` hub → town hub → service-in-town page. Each service-in-town page links **up** to its parent service page (`/services/[service]`), **across** to 2–3 genuinely-nearby town pages, and the town hub links to all its services.
- **Slug rules:** lowercase, hyphens, descriptive, keyword-front, no stop-words.
- Note: the existing dynamic `[localPage]` scaffold + `local-pages.ts` give us the data-driven foundation; we extend the data model to carry the per-town **unique** fields from §3 (intro, localFaqs, localProof, nearbyTowns, housingNote, etc.) so uniqueness is first-class, not an afterthought.

## 6. Local SEO signals — set expectations honestly

**The pages alone won't win the map pack in far towns.** For "service + town" the
**Google Business Profile + proximity + reviews** drive the map pack, and a single
Burgess-Hill-based engineer is **proximity-disadvantaged** for somewhere like Hove
(9 mi) or Lancing (12 mi). Honest expectation:
- **Organic blue-link + "near me" long-tail:** the location pages can genuinely compete here, especially in nearer towns.
- **Map pack in distant towns:** hard for a single-location SAB; don't promise map-pack dominance everywhere like Burgess Hill.

**Levers that actually move local rankings (do these alongside the pages):**
- **GBP service-area** set to the target towns; correct categories; **regular posts**.
- **Reviews that name towns** — ask customers to mention their town ("Jamie serviced our boiler in Hassocks"). This is gold for local relevance and AI/maps.
- **NAP consistency + citations** — Checkatrade, TrustATrader, MyBuilder, Bark, Yell (also in the teardown's authority plan). Being strong *on the directories that rank* is itself a channel.

## 7. Schema (deterministic, no fakery)
- **Service-in-town page:** `Service` (serviceType = the service, `areaServed` = that town, `provider` → the business `@id`) + `BreadcrumbList`.
- **Town hub:** `BreadcrumbList` + a simple service `ItemList`/`CollectionPage`.
- **AggregateRating stays at business level only** — never invent per-town ratings/reviews.

---

## 8. Technical & indexing rollout
- Pages are **SSG/prerendered** (via the `[localPage]` route) — content in initial HTML.
- **Sitemap in waves:** add Tier 1 URLs only; add later waves as they ship. Do **not** dump 130 URLs day one.
- **Watch GSC Coverage as the early-warning system:** if Google leaves pages as **"Crawled – currently not indexed"** or **"Discovered – not indexed,"** that is the signal it sees them as low-value → **stop, fix, don't push more waves**.
- **Quality-floor guard:** a page missing its unique blocks / under the word bar does not get added to the sitemap.

---

## 9. Measurement & the kill-switch
- **Baseline first:** current Burgess Hill rankings, GSC impressions/positions, indexed-page count (capture before Tier 1 ships).
- **Per-wave KPIs:** indexation rate (indexed ÷ published), rankings for the town×service queries, organic clicks to these pages, enquiries/calls attributed (GA4 events already wired).
- **PAUSE / KILL criteria (commit these):**
  - Tier 1 indexation < ~60% after 6–8 weeks → pages seen as thin → **stop & remediate**.
  - Any **site-wide** organic decline (Burgess Hill terms slipping) → possible quality demotion → **pause all expansion, investigate**.
  - GSC manual action / spam flag → **halt, full review**.

---

## 10. Risk register
1. **Scaled-content / doorway flag** (templated pages) → genuine uniqueness + phasing + culling.
2. **Site-wide quality demotion** (thin pages drag the domain) → quality floor + GSC watch + don't over-publish. _Scariest risk._
3. **Map-pack proximity ceiling** in far towns → set expectations; lean on GBP + reviews.
4. **Sole-trader content capacity** → phasing + realistic scope (likely < 130).
5. **Fabrication temptation** (fake local proof) → forbidden, full stop.

Meanwhile JDH wins now on long-tail "[service] [town]" + the softer CP12 terms where page quality beats a thin templated page.

## 1. Category conventions TO HONOUR (break these = lose trust)
- **Gas Safe registration shown prominently** (logo + number) — non-negotiable trust signal; every credible site shows it.
- **Click-to-call phone number in the header**, persistent. Trades visitors phone.
- **Service area named explicitly** (towns) — local intent + trust.
- **Real reviews / star rating** surfaced (Checkatrade/Google).
- **Clear service list** (servicing / repair / gas safety).
- **"Fully insured" / credentials** trust line.
- **Fast, obvious contact path** — phone, and increasingly WhatsApp for trades.

## 2. Category clichés TO BREAK (lazy defaults = differentiation opportunity)
- **Faceless company.** Almost every local rival hides the actual engineer (B.Cooke, Smart Heat = corporate/anonymous). → JDH shows **Jamie's face + name** everywhere.
- **Hidden pricing** ("competitive rates, no hidden fees" with no numbers). → JDH leads with the **real £75 offer** + honest "contact for a quote".
- **Reviews on Google but not on-site.** → JDH surfaces the 5.0×24 **on-page with schema**.
- **Generic stock/clip-art heating imagery.** → JDH uses **real job photos**.
- **Cluttered, dated, multi-trade plumber sites** (blue gradients, busy). → JDH goes **clean, modern, confident**.
- **"24/7 emergency" claims** everyone makes. → JDH is honest (standard hours) and competes on care, not false availability.

## 3. Trust-signal matrix (ranked by weight to THIS audience)
1. **Gas Safe registration + number** (977838) — safety/legal table-stakes, highest weight (YMYL).
2. **Real Google reviews / 5.0 rating** — social proof, very high.
3. **A real, named, visible engineer** — humanises a stranger entering your home; underused = high differentiation.
4. **Local proof** (named towns, local reviews) — "he's one of us".
5. **Real photos of actual work** — competence evidence.
6. **Transparent pricing** — reduces friction/anxiety.
7. **Fully insured** — moderate (expected, reassuring) — *pending confirmation*.
8. Manufacturer accreditation (Worcester/Vaillant) — moderate — *pending confirmation*.

## 4. Conversion pattern that wins this vertical
Visitor arrives with high local intent (often from the Google local pack) → needs to confirm **(a) legit & safe, (b) covers my town, (c) easy to reach** → converts by **phoning or messaging**, not filling long forms. So: **persistent click-to-call + WhatsApp**, trust signals above the fold, town confirmation, short enquiry form as the fallback (name/phone/postcode/service). The £75 offer is the acquisition hook. No accounts, no checkout.

## 5. Copy voice brief
Plain, first-person, safety-first, reassuring, specific. **Works (from corpus):** A.Ridley "Friendly, professional and personal service"; JDH's own "took his time on our boiler". **Fails:** "Your number one trusted provider of gas services" (B.Cooke-style filler), "Experts in the repair and replacement of Boilers across West Sussex" (Elite, generic). 3 headlines that work: clear+local+human. 3 that don't: superlative-stuffed, faceless, keyword-dumped.

## 6. Visual aesthetic brief
- **Category current-year look (from BOXT/adjacent):** big bold sans headlines, generous whitespace, **full-bleed real photography**, one confident accent colour, transparent pricing, minimal nav, sticky CTA. 
- **What looks dated (most local rivals):** blue/silver gradients, busy multi-column layouts, clip-art, tiny cramped type, carousel hero, no whitespace.
- **Craft lessons from ceiling refs (transferable):**
  - **Stripe** — proof and density handled with calm: generous section padding, restrained palette, content pinned to a strong grid, imagery does the talking. Borrow: confident whitespace + a clean asymmetric proof section.
  - **Linear** — typographic confidence + quiet motion (subtle 8–16px fade-ups), dark/ink anchored sections, no decoration clutter. Borrow: ink-dark hero or CTA band + Tier-2 motion.
  - **Airbnb/Monzo** — warm human imagery + rounded, friendly UI building trust. Borrow: lead with the **human (Jamie)**, rounded cards, warm not corporate.
- **JDH direction:** clean, light, modern, confident; ink-dark (#0B1220) + electric-blue (#2563EB) from the logo; real photography full-bleed; one accent; generous whitespace; rounded, tactile cards; Gas Safe yellow reserved strictly for the badge.

## 7. Module expectation map
- **Must-have (omission = amateur):** header w/ click-to-call, hero (who/what/where + CTA), Gas Safe trust signal, services, reviews, service area, contact path, footer w/ NAP.
- **Table-stakes (most have):** "why choose", FAQ, about snippet.
- **Differentiation opportunities (rare but effective here):** named-engineer "meet Jamie" module with real portrait; transparent £75 offer band; real-work gallery from IG; boiler-brands strip; WhatsApp CTA; answer-first CP12 content.
- **Anti-patterns (common, hurts):** hero carousel, stock imagery, "24/7" false claims, hidden pricing, faceless about page, footer keyword dump.

## 8. The three ceiling references
- **Stripe** — top-decile because restraint scales trust: nothing shouts, the grid + whitespace + proof do the work. Transferable: a calm, confident proof section (reviews + Gas Safe + photos) instead of a noisy trust bar.
- **Linear** — top-decile typographic + motion discipline: ink sections, big type, sub-200ms fade-ups, zero clutter. Transferable: the ink CTA band + Tier-2 scroll reveals + display-weight headlines.
- **Airbnb** — top-decile human-warmth + trust UX: real people, rounded friendly components, frictionless contact. Transferable: lead with Jamie's face; warm, rounded, approachable UI for a stranger-in-your-home service.

## 9. Contrarian thesis (the move no local rival makes)
**"Meet your actual engineer before he's in your home."** Every competitor sells a faceless *company*; JDH sells *Jamie* — name, face, story, Gas Safe number, real photos of his real work — making the homepage feel like a personal introduction, not a directory listing. For a safety service where a stranger enters your house, **radical personal transparency** is the differentiator. Defended: it's true (sole trader), it's already his social-proof edge (5.0×24, named reviews), and it's the one thing a multi-engineer firm structurally *cannot* copy.
